Casters are the backbone of keeping the party topped off and providing some insane party wide damage buffs. Their core damage actions are also incredibly powerful in their own ways. Majority of people will suggest **Lost Chainspell** to newer players. It provides decent enough damage buff while granting full mobility due to the instant casts
it provides. On the other hand, some will suggest bringing **Font of Magic** for it's incredibly strong damage buff. However, with **Font of Magic** you run the risk of running out of MP. So to supplement you *must* bring **Resistance Ether Kits** to help mitigate the MP costs.

Your **Pure Essence of the Elder** synergizes with **Lost Cure 4** to grant anyone affected a **10% Bravery damage buff**. It cannot understated how strong this synergy is. Because ontop of that damage buff with Endwalker's release and the squishing of stats, it has turned **Lost Cure 4** into a powerhouse of a healing
tool too. Essentially giving the party a benediction each time it's pressed. So, every time the party takes damage you should be pressing **Lost Cure 4** to help your healers top the party off and refresh everyone's bravery timer. 

:::tip[Lost Bravery v. Cure 4]
**Lost Bravery**, the castable 10m damage buff, only provides a 5% damage increase compared to Elder Cure 4's 10%. There is no reason to be using **Lost Bravery** inside of Delubrum Savage unless you are doing some sort of challenge run.
:::

Each holster is split into two sections, one as a "pre-load"  holster and an "instance ready" holster. The "pre-load" is for when you first get into the instance. It will contain your pure essence, reraiser, and actions that you will slot in or refresh charges on.
The "instance ready" holster has _everything_ you will need for the entire instance. It should often contain extra reraisers, spare copies of lost actions you need, and light curtains. It is strongly encouraged that you fill your holster to as close as full as you can.

Some suggestions on what to include to fill space: **Light Curtains**, extra **Resistance Reraisers**, and **Resistance Potions**.

:::note[Pure Essences]
You will only need **one** pure essence each time you enter Delubrum Reginae. Essences do persist through death and are only removed if you remove the buff yourself or you leave the instance.
:::

### Chainspell Cure 4
The basline caster holster. Packing both **Chainspell** and **Cure 4** for the whole instance as it's just that shrimple. **Chainspell** has 20 uses so it will last you the entire instance, no need to bring a second copy of it! As a reminder, please make sure to drift **Chainspell** into the 2 minute buff window as you want to stack
as many buffs as possible into that short timeframe. **Chainspell** does increase the cost of your spells, but it's nothing that using Lucid Dreaming on cooldown won't fix.

Use **Lucid Dreaming** whenever and also try to keep high uptime on your **Ether Kits** when possible. If you're lucky, a healer will use **Lost Fullcure** and that will solve a majority of your MP problems. But do not bank on this
during an anyprog environment.

### Basic Lost Flare Star
The almighty **Lost Flare Star** is the _most powerful_ role in the instance (outside of the duelist). Only **one** person can bring it as any others will just overwrite each other. The Flare Star's job is to be putting out as much damage as they can no matter what.
Any and all party buffs should be funneled into you. **Font of Magic** is generally the only viable option, outside of extra overslotting, to pair with **Lost Flare Star** as **Chainspell** increases the cost to an unobtainable amount of MP.

It cannot be stressed enough that you should **not** be using **Lost Chainspell** when using **Flare Star.** It makes **Flare Star** unuseable when under the effects of **Chainspell** due to the increased MP cost.

:::caution[How To Mimic]
<ActionIcon icon="mimic"/>**Mimic** is used to copy the actions of another player _and_ fully refresh your actions at the same time! To use it, first slot in <ActionIcon icon="mimic"/>**Mimic** and target the player you wish to copy the actions from.
**Before you use <ActionIcon icon="mimic"/>Mimic**, double check their **Reminiscence** buff! Doing this will ensure you are getting the correct actions. Once ready, use <ActionIcon icon="mimic"/>**Mimic!** After a nifty animation, you'll recieve **both** actions the target had and full charges of both!

**Extra note:** The target will not be affected in anyway. This is how many Delubrum Savage reclears will often smuggle heavier action combinations through the entire instance.
:::

### Excellence Cure 4 Caster
This is truly the easiest and most effective way to output as much DPS as you can on **any role**, outside of one exception. While this is a rather heavy and expensive bag, it's effects cannot be understated. Mimic is used before every major Non-Trinity Seeker fight to
refresh **Font of Power** and get **Lost Excellence** back. 

During the opener, you will press **Lost Excellence** after your first GCD. You will open your holster, select **Lost Cure 4**, and slot it [the banner] over Excellence. Once done, you can press **Font of Power** and **Lost Cure 4**
together. From there, the fight will proceed as normal with you pressing Font/Banner during every **2 minute** raid buff window.

It is important that you overslot **Excellence** with your **Lost Cure 4**. Holding **Excellence** through the whole instance is a massive DPS loss and also could potentially waste the assigned batteries's Excellence plates. 

**Reminder:** Mimic does not work in combat.

### Magical Excellence Battery
This holster replaces a **Mimic** with **Lost Excellence** to allow other players to use Mimic on you. Three players will be volunteer/assigned to carry an extra plate of Excellence to slot in at an assigned fight.
One will carry it for Queen's Guard, Trinity Avowed, and The Queen. Once you have arrived at your assigned fight: slot in **Lost Excellence** over **Lost Cure 4** (keeping **Font of Magic** still) and inform the raid with a message or marking yourself with a triangle head marker to
signify that you are ready to be mimicked.

During the opener, you will press **Lost Excellence** after your first GCD. You will open your holster, select **Lost Cure 4**, and slot it [cure4] over Excellence. Once done, you can press **Font of Magic** and **Lost Cure 4**
together. From there, the fight will go on with you pressing Font/cure4 during every **2 minute** raid buff window as you normally would.
